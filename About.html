About Me

I’ve always been drawn to how things work, from taking apart small gadgets as a kid to designing complete systems as a mechanical engineering student at Tufts. Over time, that curiosity evolved into my passion for mechanical design and mechatronics, where I get to bring hardware, software, and manufacturing together to create real, functional products.

I’m excited about the intersection of design, technology, and user experience, especially where engineering can make products more intuitive, accessible, or meaningful in people’s lives. Whether it’s in tech, health, or manufacturing, I’m motivated by the impact well-designed hardware can have on the world around us.

Outside the lab, I stay active through Club Water Polo, intramural basketball, and distance running. I love meeting new people and spending as much time outdoors as possible, whether that's going to the beach with friends and family or taking my dog on hikes in Vermont.
